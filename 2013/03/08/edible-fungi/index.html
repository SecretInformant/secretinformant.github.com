Edible Fungi of East Donyland

The running order is derived from Philips "Guide To Mushrooms and Toadstools"

While this may be an exhausting list of species (for Rowhdege), it is not exhaustive. There remain a dozen or more that are future possibilities. They either haven't turned up yet, or I haven't been inclined to try them. I am not, despite opinions to the contrary, reckless in my pursuit of new fungal experiences. Many "edible" species are passed up if not deemed to be worthwhile, i.e., I aim for the "Quite good" or better characterisation. The scoring is entirely subjective and therefore not necessarily in agreement with the published authorities . This maybe because I may not yet have found optimum ways of cooking each type. It is noticable that many famed chefs' recipes do not use different cooking times, or methods, for specific wild mushrooms. This is clearly incorrect.

Notes:-

{note 1} Although these Amanitas are edible and quite good I usually heed the advice to by-pass the group because of the dangerous, even deadly, members of the family. Death Cap (Amanita phalloides), for instance is regularly seen hereabouts.

{note 2} Although cooked and eaten in Rowhedge these were gathered in what is strictly West Donyland/Friday Woods.

{note 3} Not worth repeating the experience.
